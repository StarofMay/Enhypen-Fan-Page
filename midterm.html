About Enhypen 𓆩^._.^𓆪


Enhypen (Korean: 엔하이픈; stylized as ENHYPEN) is a South Korean boy band formed through the survival show "I-LAND." The group debuted on November 30, 2020, under Belift Lab, a joint venture between Big Hit Entertainment and CJ ENM. Enhypen consists of seven members: Jungwon, Heeseung, Jay, Jake, Sunghoon, Sunoo, and Ni-ki. The group quickly gained popularity for their unique concept, powerful performances, and strong vocal abilities.

The name Enhypen was introduced during the live broadcast of the final episode of I-Land. Etymologically, the group derives their name from the hyphen symbol (-), representing "Connection, Discovery, & Growth". Similar to how a hyphen connects different words to create new meanings, Enhypen aims "to come together to connect, discover and grow together to form a new act".

Engene is the official fandom name for Enhypen. The name was announced on October 9th, 2020. The name holds two significant meanings: "Engene" as a play on words, representing fans as the engines that help the group grow and move forward, and also as the fans being the gene that shares the same DNA as ENHYPEN, connecting them to discover and grow together.

ENHYPEN's concept centers on a dark, romantic narrative of vampires and werewolves, particularly in their "DARK MOON" universe, which explores themes of love, desire, and connection between these mythical beings and their human reincarnations. Their music and lore, originating from the survival show I-LAND, often feature storylines of immortal beings navigating new worlds, grappling with their past, and forming bonds with humans. This unique concept has captivated fans and set them apart in the K-pop industry.
